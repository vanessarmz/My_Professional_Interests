Data Storage and Management

A certain set of skills and qualifications are required to become a data storage professional. The most important of them is a thorough understanding of Data Storage, which includes knowledge of architecture and performance difficulties. It is critical to understand EMC technologies like as capacity planning, provisioning, and Symmetrix Remote Data Facility. A thorough understanding of Storage Solutions and Disaster Recovery techniques identifies a qualified data storage specialist. Knowledge of Linux, an operating system often used in data storage situations, is also useful. Advanced knowledge of database management systems, hands-on expertise in database design and development, and proficiency in SQL and other database-specific programming languages are required. Furthermore, knowledge of data modeling and data warehousing improves one's talents in this industry. A Data Storage Specialist's responsibilities are diverse and might vary depending on the position, company, or sector. These tasks include delivering and administering storage systems, controlling performance and fine-tuning, and performing live host migrations across platforms such as Linux.
